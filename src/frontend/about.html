"Watch a Dutch town navigate a healthcare system that's breaking."

What Is This?

This is a simulation of the Dutch healthcare crisis. 5,000 AI citizens live, get sick, queue for care, and sometimes die — not from disease alone, but from a system that can't keep up.

Every citizen has real demographics from CBS, real disease prevalence from RIVM, and navigates a system governed by real NZa consultation rates and Treeknorm waiting limits. Toggle between IST (current reality) and SOLL (AI-native future) to see the difference technology can make.

How It Works

Two Futures, One Town

⚠ IST Mode (Current Reality)

  • GPs lose 30% of time to admin → effective capacity 62%
  • Wait times exceed Treeknorm → patients deteriorate
  • HP drain accelerates with wait time and severity
  • When HP reaches 0 → ghost sprite (preventable death)
drainRate × (wait - Treeknorm) × severity × (1 + adminBurden)

✦ SOLL Mode (AI-Native Future)

  • Admin drops to <5% → effective capacity 83%
  • AI triage reduces wait to <4 weeks
  • 34% efficiency multiplier from AI assistance
  • Fewer preventable deaths, more treatments completed
Same citizens, same diseases — better system outcomes

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Is this real data?
Yes. Demographics match CBS 2024, disease prevalence matches RIVM, costs match NZa tariffs. Every parameter in the simulation is sourced from official Dutch healthcare statistics.
Why do people die?
Not from disease alone — from system delay. The HP drain formula accelerates when wait times exceed the Treeknorm (legal maximum wait). A patient waiting 12 weeks for a 4-week-maximum referral loses HP rapidly, especially with severe conditions or comorbidities.
What's the difference between IST and SOLL?
IST = current Dutch healthcare system with 30% admin burden and long waits. SOLL = AI-native transformation where admin drops to 5% and AI triage keeps waits under the Treeknorm. Toggle between them to see the impact on the same population.
Can I use this for research?
Yes! Export data as JSON/CSV from the Stats panel (📊) in the simulation. The simulation generates FHIR R4-compliant medical records for every citizen.
How many citizens are simulated?
5,000 AI citizens with realistic age distributions, each capable of developing up to 10 real medical conditions via Markov-chain disease progression. They have names, biographies, cognitive loops, and individual care pathways.
