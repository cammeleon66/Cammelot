The Origin Story

A 16-Bit Mirror for a 300,000-Worker Shortage

By 2035, the Netherlands will face a shortage of 301,000 healthcare workers. We talk about AI as a magic wand, but we rarely define the spells. I built Cammelot to move the conversation from "vibes" to "variables."

I have worked in Big Tech for the last six years. You should assume I’m biased toward AI. I’ve seen the demos where Large Language Models draft perfect referral letters in seconds. It looks like the future. But if you talk to a GP in a Brabant village or a nurse in a Rotterdam ER, they don’t see a "copilot." They see a system that is fundamentally out of breath.

That disconnect is why I built Cammelot. It isn’t a product; it’s a digital twin of a Dutch town. It’s built with 16-bit pixels, but it is powered by 2025 RIVM disease prevalence data and NZa tariffs. It features 45 citizens, 3 GPs, and 1 hospital. They don’t just walk around; they age, develop chronic conditions, and—if the system fails them—they turn into ghosts.

"The biggest drop in mortality didn't come from 'smart diagnosis'—it came from giving clinicians 25% of their day back by automating paperwork."

The Hendrik Problem

Meet Hendrik. In the simulation, he’s 70 with moderate COPD. In the Status Quo (IST), Hendrik enters a 12-week waiting list. His GP is at 120% capacity, drowning in 30% administrative overhead. Hendrik doesn't get a proactive check-up. He gets an exacerbation. He becomes a ghost.

In the AI-Native (SOLL) mode, we don't give the GP "magic goggles." We give them time. By automating the FHIR-based handshakes between agents, the system catches Hendrik’s decline two weeks earlier. He stays a pixel-citizen.

From Vibes to Variables

Cammelot is an Agentic Mesh. Every GP and Hospital is an Autonomous Agent. They negotiate in real-time: "Who has the capacity? Who meets the Treeknorm?"

After 200 simulation runs, the data taught me three things that "AI optimists" often ignore:

  • Detection is Useless Without Capacity: An alert means nothing if the GP is at 120% capacity.
  • The Efficiency Trap: Optimizing for "throughput" accidentally hurts complex elderly patients. We built "Fairness Guardrails."
  • Admin Relief is the Hero: The most lives were saved by automating paperwork, not by "AI doctors."
